# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Property owners and landlords managing their own rental houses. They work from home or a small office, often on a laptop, checking occupancy status, recording meter readings, generating invoices, and tracking payments. They are not tech-power-users — they want something that feels familiar and obvious, not something they need to learn. Thai-language interface throughout; the audience is Thai-native.

## Product Purpose

RMS Manager is a rental property management system for small-scale landlords. It handles the full lifecycle of rental operations: room inventory, tenant onboarding, contracts, meter readings (water/electric), invoice generation, payment tracking, and maintenance requests. Success means a landlord can manage 5–50 units without spreadsheets, phone notes, or paper ledgers — everything in one screen, fast and correct.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, Approachable, Simple. The system should feel like a helpful assistant, not a corporate tool. Warm but not childish. Clear but not cold. It earns trust through clarity and speed, not through visual complexity or feature density.

## Anti-references

- Overly complex enterprise tools (SAP, Oracle-style density) — too much information, too many panels, intimidating for a single landlord
- Generic SaaS dashboards with bland gray corporate aesthetics — lifeless, forgettable
- Developer-facing dark-mode tools — wrong audience entirely

## Design Principles

1. **Show the answer, not the controls.** The most common task (checking room status, seeing who owes money) should be answered at a glance, not behind a click.
2. **One thing per screen, done well.** Each page has a single clear purpose. Resist the urge to combine unrelated data on one view.
3. **Friendly confidence.** The interface should feel warm and helpful — like talking to a knowledgeable friend — not sterile or intimidating.
4. **Forgive mistakes.** Landlords enter data in a hurry. Make undo easy, confirmation clear, and destructive actions hard to trigger by accident.
5. **Speed is respect.** A landlord managing 20 rooms shouldn't wait. Fast loads, instant feedback, no unnecessary steps.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Body text contrast ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1. Keyboard-navigable for all interactive elements. Visible focus states. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations. Thai text must remain legible at all sizes — test with Kanit at the intended weights.
